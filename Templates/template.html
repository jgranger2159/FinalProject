Review Rating Predictor!

The aim of this project was to create a model that could successfully predict the rating that a given review received. It is most suitable to be used with electronics reviews, which is what it was trained on.

This was done by using a large dataset of Amazon reviews of electronics, collected by Johns Hopkins University. The dataset contained "positive" reviews(4 and 5 stars) and "negative" reviews(1 and 2 stars). The data was processed and tokenized with NLTK, the Natural Language Toolkit, and lemmatized with WordNet to help simplify calculations. The tokens were hashed into "positive" and "negative" sets, then put through a sigmoid function to obtain a "semantics score", between -1 and 1, that describes how weighted the word was toward being in positive or negative reviews. These scores are all placed into a database with their corresponding token.

Each review entered is run through a processor that will remove insignificant words(less than 2 letters), stop words(list obtained from NLTK), and lemmatized with NLTK's WordNet functionality. Then each token is compared to the database and the semantic score is summed up to create a number that describes how positive or negative the review is. This is then compared to the reviews that the model already knows and the prediction is given.

The largest and most glaring weakness of the model is the inability to predict 3 star ratings. The dataset that I was working with did not contain any 3 star ratings because they are "neutral", and therefore do not contribute to building the model. Additionally, while the model is very accurate at predicting a "positive" or "negative" review (79%, high for semantics analysis), it does a pretty poor job of predicting the correct rating(52%, pretty mediocre). The model does not account for the length of the review. For instance, a 4 star review that is 100 words long may be incorrectly labeled as a 5 star review because of how the score tallies up. In the same way, a resoundingly positive but short 5 star review (e.g. "Excellent!") may be given a 4 star rating.
